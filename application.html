Cost-Effectiveness Overview

Despite a higher initial cost, automated systems offer clear long-term advantages in labor savings, 24/7 operation, and user satisfaction — ideal for medium to large facilities.

Data Speaks: Case Studies

Case 1. Operational Cost Gap – Singapore Hospital

Study comparing 3 systems (Manual, LED-guided, LED+Lock-controlled):

Manual: ~S$60,912/year
LED-guided: ~S$129,832/year
LED + Lock: ~S$152,894/year

Medication Errors Avoided:
LED-guided: 365 events
LED + Lock: 658 events

Cost per avoided error:
LED-guided: S$189
LED + Lock: S$140

≈ US$140–190 per event avoided (depending on facility size).

Sources: tjps.pharm.chula.ac.th, PMC, ResearchGate

Case 2. 5-Year Cost Comparison – Canadian Report

Based on a CADTH report over 5 years:

Automated System: $816k–$840k
Manual System: $968k
➤ Savings: $128k–$152k (13–16%)

Labor Cost Share:
Manual: 68%
Automated: 50%

Pharmacist/Technician Ratio:
Manual: 26%
Automated: 13%

Equipment Setup:
~20% of total cost (Automated)

Sources: PMC, cda-amc.ca, Canadian Society of Hospital Pharmacists

Conclusion: Although automated systems cost 1.3–2× more upfront, they save 10–20% over five years. Each avoided error saves ~US$100–200.
